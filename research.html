Energy Storage and Flexible Generation Resources

Exploiting the flexibility of energy storage and quick-start units

The flexibility of storage and quick-start generators to adjust in near real-time operations is appealing in the high renewable era. However, a sizable body of planning and day-ahead unit commitment models haven't adequately considered this feature in the stochastic/robust optimization context. In a series of works (TPWRS 2019; TPWRS 2022a; TII 2022), I and collaborators analyzed the benefits of modeling the aforementioned feature in the recourse problems. We also explored several approach to solve the resultant challenging stochastic/robust optimization with mixed-integer recourse.

[TPWRS 2019] Siyuan Wang, Guangchao Geng, and Quanyuan Jiang, “Robust Co-Planning of Energy Storage and Transmission Line with Mixed Integer Recourse,” IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, vol. 34, no. 6, pp. 4728-4738, 2019. [Link] [PDF]

[TPWRS 2022a] Siyuan Wang, Chaoyue Zhao, Lei Fan, and Rui Bo, “Distributionally Robust Unit Commitment with Flexible Generation Resources Considering Renewable Energy Uncertainty,” IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, to appear. [Link] [PDF]

[TII 2022] Siyuan Wang, Guangchao Geng, Quanyuan Jiang, and Rui Bo, “Generation Expansion Planning Considering Discrete Storage Model and Renewable Energy Uncertainty: A Bi-Interval Optimization Approach,” IEEE Transactions on Industrial Informatics, to appear.

Enhaced modeling for pumped storage hydro

Accurately modeling pumped storage hydro (PSH) flexibility is important as the penetration of renewable energy increases. In a series of papers (TPWRS 2021; TPWRS 2022b; CAIE 2021; JES 2022), I and collaborators proposed enhanced PSH modeling approaches that can reflect the detailed state transitions and practical input-output curve (which describes the relation of power, flow rate, and water head) with accelerated computation time. We also implemented our model for real-world Ludington PSH power plant.

[TPWRS 2021] Siyuan Wang, Jian Liu, Haotian Chen, Rui Bo, and Yonghong Chen, “Modeling State Transition and Head-Dependent Efficiency Curve for Pumped Storage Hydro in Look-Ahead Dispatch,” IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, vol. 36, no. 6, pp. 5396-5407, 2021. [Link] [PDF]

[TPWRS 2022b] Siyuan Wang, Jian Liu, Rui Bo, and Yonghong Chen, “Approximating Input-Output Curve of Pumped Storage Hydro Plant: A Disjunctive Convex Hull Method,” IEEE Transactions on Power Systems, to appear. [Link] [PDF]

[CAIE 2021] Jian Liu, Rui Bo, Siyuan Wang, and Haotian Chen, “Optimal Scheduling for Profit Maximization Energy Storage Merchants Considering Market Impact Based on Dynamic Programming,” Computers & Industrial Engineering, vol. 155, pp. 107212, 2021. [Link] [PDF]

[JES 2022] Jian Liu, Xin-yue Sun, Rui Bo, Siyuan Wang, and Meng Ou, “Economic Dispatch for Electricity Merchant with Energy Storage and Wind Plant: State of Charge Based Decision Making Considering Market Impact and Uncertainties,” Journal of Energy Storage, to appear.

Power and Hydrogen Systems

Exploring storage capability in hydrogen supply chains

The storage capability of hydrogen supply chains can potentially defer transmission investment with expected cost reduction of electrolyzers in the long-term future. In a recent work (TIA 2021), I and collaborator developed novel compact truck routing and seasonal storage models. We use these models to quantify the hydrogen system flexibility and analyze synergistic benefits from a power transmission expansion perspective.

[TIA 2021] Siyuan Wang, and Rui Bo, “Joint Planning of Electricity Transmission and Hydrogen Transportation Networks,” IEEE Transactions on Industry Applications, vol. 58, no. 2, pp. 2887-2897, 2022. [Link] [PDF]
